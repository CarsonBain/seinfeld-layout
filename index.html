Seinfeld

Overview

Overview

In my opinion, Seinfeld is the best sitcom on TV, period. It's not a show that involves an intricate plot or developed story line that evolves over a series of episodes, instead each episode is completely different from the episode before it relying on small, seemingly pointless events tying into eachother to create the main narrative. It's a show that has been famously described as "the show about nothing", focusing on the comedy in human interaction in every-day life.

Created by Jerry Seinfeld and Larry David, the show stars Jerry Seinfeld playing a character based closely on himself and follows him and his neurotic friends through their day-to-day experiences -- many based on the writer's real life experiences. I think what makes this show so great is how relatable and unpredictable it can be. While the characters and situations may have seemed a bit outlandish, it's easy to envision yourself being in a similar situation. For someone looking to get into Seinfeld but doesn't know where to start, check out this article of all 169 episodes with plot summaries and rankings from best to worst.

The Opposite

The Seinfeld episode titled "The Opposite" is definitely my favourite of all 169 episodes of the series. The episode revolves around one of the main characters named George attempting to turn his life around by doing the exact opposite of what he would usually do. It turns out that this method is extremely beneficial to George and he ends up getting a new girlfriend, a new job and a new apartment. What I love about this episode is how relatable it is -- who hasn't had a string of bad luck and thought about doing the exact same thing. The fact that it ends up working out so well for George (for the most part) makes it very satisfying to watch even though the concept is so ridiculous.
